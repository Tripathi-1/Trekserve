APR 2, 2018

As many as 12 million–20 million people in Egypt—almost a quarter of its population—live in informal housing. Yet, in urban areas alone, about 3 million homes are left empty by their owners, or unfinished. To help change this, in 2014 the Egyptian government launched a Social Housing Program, which aims to provide a million new housing units, including better homes for low-income families. Every year, Egypt needs to build about 300,000 new homes for newly formed households. At the moment, it also needs 254,000 more to catch up with a housing backlog that’s accumulated over the past five years.
